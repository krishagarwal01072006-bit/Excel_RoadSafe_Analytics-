# Excel_RoadSafe_Analytics-
RoadSafe Analytics – UK Road Accident Casualty Intelligence Dashboard
📌 What It Is:

A comprehensive Excel-based analytics dashboard built on 307,973 real road accident records covering 2024–2025
Tracks 417,883 total casualties across severity levels — Fatal, Serious, and Slight — with full geographic and contextual breakdown
Covers key variables including accident severity, vehicle type, road type, road surface, weather conditions, and urban vs rural classification
Designed to provide traffic authorities and road safety teams a single unified view of accident patterns across the UK


🔧 What I Did:

Cleaned and structured a large-scale raw accident dataset of 300,000+ records for reliable analysis
Built an interactive dashboard with KPI cards, monthly trend charts, donut charts, tree maps, and slicers for dynamic filtering
Performed year-over-year comparison (2024 vs 2025) to identify rising or declining casualty trends
Segmented data across vehicle type, road type, surface condition, lighting, and area type for multi-dimensional insights


❗ What Problem It Solved:

Road safety teams lack a quick, visual way to identify where, when, and why accidents happen at scale
Traditional accident reports are static, slow, and unable to highlight patterns across hundreds of thousands of records
There was no unified view connecting casualty severity, road conditions, and vehicle behavior in one interactive place
This dashboard solved all three by creating a centralized accident intelligence system for faster, evidence-based safety decisions


📈 Impact & Outcome:

Revealed that Single Carriageways account for 74% of all accidents — a direct input for infrastructure investment decisions
Found that Urban areas record nearly double the casualties of Rural zones — guiding where safety campaigns should be focused
Identified Cars as the dominant vehicle type in accidents, helping traffic policy teams prioritize enforcement strategies
Showed that Slight casualties (263,280) vastly outnumber Fatal (3,953) — enabling tiered response planning for emergency services
